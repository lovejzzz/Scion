## Honest readings

1. **Trellis wins quality on virgin ground** (8.33) at 3× the Compiler's
   cost. The Compiler is remarkably efficient on mini ($0.07) but keeps its
   signature "graded A, judged 5–6" templating — reproduced perfectly on a
   course it had never seen.
2. **The E2B-MAX pipeline's day-one weakness is PROSE, not items.** Its items
   are solver-verified and measured at mini-parity — but researcher-zero's
   first-touch surfaces (extractive + small-model skin) judge at 4.33 on
   virgin ground, and the classroom battery flags thin catching depth
   (repair 0.339 vs bar 0.7 — only ~28 items on day one). Prior zero courses
   judged 6.67 with richer/deeper deposits: the number grows with the bank,
   and the replay price never moves from $0.
3. **The cold-start cascade worked end-to-end under the mini constraint:**
   empty genome → 7 kernels deposited → mini authored the wrong beliefs the
   sources didn't state (the documented paid top-up, $0.034) → E2B-MAX
   authored 28 items, solver verified → aliases + relink bound the frozen
   graph to the new kernels → 96/A shipped at $0.
4. **Live-wire fixes found by the bake-off** (all committed): the paid
   misconception top-up path; kernel aliases as the relink contract (a
   non-stemming matcher needs them); the dedupe-triggered escalation
   self-diversifies item top-ups on re-runs.

## Where this leaves the decision

For a brand-new course requested ONCE: Trellis (quality) or Compiler (price).
For a course that will be generated, regenerated, synced, and served offline
repeatedly — the E2B-MAX pipeline's $0.05-once/$0-forever economics dominate
after ~1 repeat vs Trellis, ~1 vs Composer, and its quality ceiling rises
with every deposit while the others' costs recur. The named next lever for

## Addendum 3 — the compiler seat: E2B-MAX as the app's ONLY LLM (July 6, night)

The other direction from arm 5: instead of the $0 pipeline, run **the classic
app compiler itself** on E2B — `crucible --llm e2b` reroutes every
api.openai.com call (playwright context.route) to
`scripts/crucible/e2bOpenAIShim.mjs`; no src/ change, no paid key, generation
never leaves the machine. Same music-theory course, same deep grader, same
3-seat judge harness (`trellis/advisoryJudge.mjs`; paid package re-judged
5.67 [6,7,4] today — the bake-off number reproduced exactly).

| Arm                              | Grade          | Judge (3 seats)  | Paid gen cost | Wall |
| -------------------------------- | -------------- | ---------------- | ------------- | ---- |
| Compiler + gpt-5.4-mini (arm 1)  | 99/A, 0 P1     | 5.67 [6,7,4]     | $0.0732       | 199s |
| **Compiler + E2B (shim, run 1)** | 98/A, **1 P1** | **3.33 [4,4,2]** | **$0.0000**   | 217s |

The P1 is the story: **every long-JSON authoring call failed the app's parse
gates and the compiler fell back to deterministic mail-merge** ("compiled
without enrichment", honestly disclosed by the v0.12.1 gates; judge notes:
"template residue", "no actual musical content"). What E2B DID hold: the
voice pass (8/8 surfaces, texture 88→100) and the native skeleton — every
sub-2K-token JSON call, plus prompt-shaped long JSON outside the app (a 7-lesson
enrichment-shaped probe: 9.4K chars, parsed first try).

Body autopsy (SHIM_BODY_LOG, 23 captured calls) pinned the failure class:
**near-miss punctuation/bracket drift at 18–44K chars** — CourseIR was valid
JSON except ONE missing comma at char 2,296; enrichment outputs die of small
bracket slips (`"]}]]`) a few thousand chars in. `json_repair` recovers the
COMPLETE CourseIR object (all 6 top-level keys) but only ~59% of enrichment.
Two compounding harness truths: greedy decoding makes the app's 4-attempt
retry ladder **byte-identical wasted work** (same defect, same position, every
retry), and full-size on-device calls are competitive single-shot (courseIR
63s vs paid 41.5s; enrichment 87s vs 60.9s) but the retry×queue spiral blew
the 10-min finalize budget once the cap was lifted (runs 2–3, no artifacts).

**Where this leaves the compiler seat:** not viable today at $0 — the
compiler's quality lives in exactly the two long-JSON calls E2B can't land
unrepaired, and the honest result is mail-merge at judge 3.33 vs paid 5.67.
Named levers, in order: (1) a grammar-aware repair tier in the shim
(jsonrepair rescues CourseIR whole — likely flips the biggest call), (2)
temperature>0 on retries so the ladder samples instead of replaying, (3)
chunked Pass B (per-lesson calls put every response in the size band E2B
already lands). None measured yet; the shim stays repair-free by design so
this table stays honest.

**What V2 built and proved (each fix keyed to a measured failure):**

1. **Grammar-constrained decoding end-to-end** (llguidance in serve_g4,
   fast-tokenizer twin, schema/jsonMode over the JSONL protocol): parse
   validity by construction. The whitespace knife-edge measured three ways —
   `""` cascades greedy into 770-char early-exit, `flexible` floods
   indentation until finish=length, `[ \n]{0,2}` lands finish=stop VALID.
2. **The kernel contract, enforced**: Pass B ships json_object with its
   shape only in prompt text; the shim derives a STRICT schema from the
   app's own contract + lint floor (facts ≥25ch, df ≥45ch, exactly-4
   options, per-lesson required atoms) and llguidance makes the acceptance
   floor the only legal shape. Round 10: **first 7/7 enrichment ever on a
   local model** — the mail-merge P1 died there.
3. **Structure pinning**: skeleton and CourseIR calls enforce the counts
   their own prompts declare (the 25-lesson greedy hallucination poisoned
   two rounds before the pin; a parse-valid-but-wrong CourseIR is worse
   than an unparseable one).
4. **Per-lesson chunking + cross-window chunk cache**: the app aborts
   provider calls at ~120s while 7 chunked generations need 5-8 min — the
   cache banks lessons across the app's own retry ladder and converges by
   attempt 2-3. Enhancement passes deliberately overrun the abort (the
   async work persists; overrun is free quality).
5. **Anti-degeneration pattern**: greedy space-runs inside string values
   ate 8K-token budgets byte-identically every round (0.37 chars/token);
   `^\S+( \S+)*$` on every string made the loop illegal — the stuck lesson
   generated in 26s.
6. **MAX² passes, all $0**: best-of-2 mc sets picked by blind-solve
   agreement (lesson-2's cursed set: agreement 1→4), two-solve tie-break
   item verification, topic gate with targeted regeneration, self-refine
   prose polish (the pass that took study guides past paid), control-char
   sanitizer (a literal ANSI sequence in an answer key read as "corruption"
   to every judge seat).
7. **Phase-0 training spike GREEN** (the roadmap's blocking unknown):
